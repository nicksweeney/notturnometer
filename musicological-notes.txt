Musicological notes — close calls in work identity
===================================================

A log of the judgment calls behind work-grouping decisions that the
canonicalization rules can't fully automate: the cases where two recordings or
titles are arguably the same work and arguably distinct, and a human had to
decide. Each entry records the case, the evidence (favour the performance-PID
fingerprint over title strings), the decision, the reasoning, and any rule it
sets or tests.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Instrument family as a work boundary  (violin/cello vs keyboard variants)
2026-06-15
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Case. Franck, Sonata in A major (M.8 / FWV.8). Composed for violin (1886);
Delsart's cello transcription (1888, Franck-sanctioned) is the standard cello
version. In the corpus it fragments 11 ways / 226 airings, on three axes:
catalogue (M.8 / FWV.8 / none), spelling, and instrument (violin / cello /
"violin or cello" / "either violin or cello").

Evidence (performance PIDs). 18 distinct recordings: 11 violin, 7 cello. The
performer credit is ALWAYS unambiguous — a cellist plays cello even under a
"violin or cello" title — whereas the segment title is not reliable for the
instrument. Durations overlap completely (violin 1530-1815s, cello 1672-1787s):
one ~27-minute work. So the instrument can only be recovered from the performer
fingerprint, never from the title.

Policy (Nick). Instrument FAMILY is the work boundary, because interchangeability
differs by family:
  - KEYBOARD (organ / piano / harpsichord / fortepiano / clavichord) — one
    player can perform one piece on any of them, so they are NOT a work
    boundary: MERGE. Precedent: Franck Prelude, Fugue et Variation, organ +
    piano merged (commit 1a531ab).
  - NON-KEYBOARD (strings, winds: violin / cello / flute / oboe / ...) — a
    cellist does not play violin; these are genuinely different instruments, so
    they ARE a work boundary: SPLIT, keyed off the performer instrument.

Consequence for M.8: should split into a Violin Sonata work (11 recs) and a
Cello Sonata work (7 recs), keyed by performer instrument — but this is DEFERRED
(see resolution): the ambiguous titles ("violin or cello") can't be split by
title, only by the performer credit, so it needs the fingerprint-aware work
oracle. M.8 stays fragmented for now rather than be force-split incoherently.

Downstream cost (measured 2026-06-15).
  - Merge keyboard: SMALL. ~7 works / 96 airings are currently split only by a
    keyboard-instrument token (mostly Haydn keyboard/piano trios + sonatas, one
    Scarlatti). Catalogued keyboard works already merge via the catalogue (§)
    path, which drops scoring wording.
  - Split non-keyboard: the scoring gate already keeps instruments distinct in
    the key, so the instrument-VARIANT axis is largely the status quo. The cost
    is the ARRANGEMENT axis: `_strip_arrangement_tail` currently folds 371
    titles / 1103 airings of "arr./transc. for <non-keyboard>" into a
    different-instrument original ("Concertino for oboe arr. for trumpet" ->
    oboe).

Open tension. M.8's cello appears on BOTH axes: as an unlabeled "Cello Sonata in
A" (instrument-variant -> split under this policy) AND as "Violin Sonata in A,
arr. for cello" (labeled arrangement -> `_strip_arrangement_tail` folds it to
violin today). The same Delsart transcription therefore lands in two different
works depending only on whether the cataloguer wrote "arr. for cello" or "Cello
Sonata".

Resolution (Nick, 2026-06-15). Scope the policy to keep the downstream coherent
and cheap:
  1. MERGE keyboard — SHIPPED now (commit below): the 7 token-sort-path keyboard
     works fold to their dominant form (6 Haydn keyboard trios/sonatas + 1
     Scarlatti). ~96 airings. Catalogued keyboard works already merged via §.
  2. SPLIT non-keyboard on the instrument-VARIANT axis only (largely the status
     quo — the scoring gate already keeps instruments distinct).
  3. LEAVE labeled arrangements ("arr./transc. for X") folding to the original.
     Do NOT make `_strip_arrangement_tail` instrument-family-aware — that would
     reverse 371 titles / 1103 airings of cross-family arrangement folds for a
     coherence edge, too much blast radius.
  4. M.8 itself: DEFER. A correct violin/cello split needs the performer
     instrument (the title is ambiguous), i.e. the parked fingerprint-aware work
     oracle assigning works per recording_pid. Until then M.8 stays as-is rather
     than force an incoherent title-only split. This makes M.8 a concrete
     motivating case for that oracle.
